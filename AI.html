In recent years, generative AI has been evolving at a remarkably fast rate. I wanted to comment on the implications this has on the role of the author. The title of the website is a way of saying that we, as a society, have killed the author by embracing AI as a tool to create texts. We are nearing a point where AI-generated texts are indistinguishable from texts written by real people. For all you know, this very text could be written by an AI.

I wanted to illustrate the effects of the emergence of AI by filling the website with several pages talking about the same simple prompt, the title of the website, but going in all sorts of different directions.

The first thing one is met with upon opening the website is the sentence "The author is dead and we have killed her." This can be viewed as a metaphor for how our collective shift towards embracing AI as a valid tool of authorship is slowly changing the role that traditional authors play in our society. It is debateable whether AI will ever be able to completely mimic the creativity that authors exhibit, but even the possibility of getting anywhere near that presents a threat to human authors.

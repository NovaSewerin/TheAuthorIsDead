I wanted to make a website that explores the role religion plays in our modern society. The title, "The Author is Dead and we Have Killed Her" is a reference to the Friedrich Nietzsche quote "God is dead, and we have killed him."

The use of the word "author" instead of God, as well as the website's monospace font, represents the advent of the printing press, as well as the enlightenment that came with it. It is those societal changes that have led to the "death" of God.

The website's title is a modified version of "God is dead, and we have killed him" - a quote from Friedrich Nietzsche displaying the way people relate to God, and religion as a whole, in our modern enlightened society.

The many subpages with different theories represent the ways people try to find meaning despite the death of God. It is fitting, then, that the last theory is that the website is about nothing at all. God truly is dead.
